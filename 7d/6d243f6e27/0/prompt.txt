### Design choice for the breaker-reset-request mechanism
**Bool flag `_breaker_reset_requested`**, consumed at the top of `_async_tick_impl`. Chose a flag over a queue because:
- Resets are idempotent and carry no payload â€” there is no distinguishing metadata between reset requests.

- Cheapest, most readable option; zero allocation cost on the hot callback path.

### Decision on the unconditional `record_failure()`
**Changed.** Removed the eager `record_failure()` at the end of `_perform_sync`'s set branch. Replaced with a deferred `record_failure()` on the post-verification-failed branch, gated on `was_set` (clears are expected to always succeed; a clear that doesn't read back is a stale-read artifact, not a real failure).
